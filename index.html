How Oracle’s $37 Billion Failure Created SENTINEL
Two documents in this Command Center tell the complete story. The first — Oracle Federal Contract Failure Record — covers 20 years across VA, DoD, GSA, and Oregon: $13.84 billion obligated for the VA EHR alone, 6 of 171 facilities live after 8 years, veterans fatally harmed, 11,000 clinical orders routed to an unknown queue, FBI investigation in Oregon, $280 million DoD HR system terminated by DOGE, 9 of 10 GAO priority recommendations unimplemented. The second — VA IT Infrastructure and Oracle Contract Analysis FY2026 — shows what happened next: Oracle’s contract was doubled to $2.2 billion while DOGE terminated the 6 veteran-owned small businesses providing support.
That pattern — a system that cannot see the veteran’s record, cannot apply the correct law, and cannot be held accountable when it fails — is structural, not incidental. That gap is what SENTINEL was built to close. The architecture Oracle was paid $37 billion to create and did not deliver — SENTINEL delivers it from the ground up, built right, built to a statutory standard, and built so no future Oracle can happen again.

HOW ORACLE’S FAILURE IGNITED SENTINEL  ·  FOUR STRUCTURAL PROBLEMS, FOUR ARCHITECTURAL SOLUTIONS
01
The Fragmentation Problem
VistA still runs at 94% of VA facilities. The two systems do not synchronize reliably. SENTINEL’s response: mandatory 15-minute synchronization ceiling between VBA and VHA databases, by statute, with automatic breach consequences if violated.
02
The Denial Pattern
Veterans denied care through citation of policies that do not apply to them. These are systemic — caused by the correct law not being on the screen when the decision is made. SENTINEL’s response: Program Coverage Schedule displays only law applicable to this veteran, this condition, this transaction.
03
The Accountability Vacuum
Oracle received $4.4 billion through FY2025 and refunded $325,000. There is no mechanism to automatically catch a bad denial and escalate without human intervention. SENTINEL’s response: four-layer VETSAFE-Grade verification, immutable audit trail by name, Inaction-as-Decision.
04
The Survivor Blind Spot
Neither Oracle’s EHR nor VistA automatically notifies a veteran’s family of benefits owed at time of death. The family must know to ask. SENTINEL’s response: Algorithm 5 Ripple Effect — 24-hour automatic notification to next of kin. Every entitlement. No claim required.
